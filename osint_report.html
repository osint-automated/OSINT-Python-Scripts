Intent: The intent of this report is to provide a high-level overview of significant military and geopolitical developments during the reporting period. The report consists of open-source reporting and updates on evolving conflicts, diplomatic initiatives, and strategic military actions involving countries of interest in the following Combatant Commands: SOUTHCOM, EUCOM, PACOM, CENTCOM, AFRICOM.

Executive Summary: During the reporting period, significant military and geopolitical developments occurred across multiple regions. In Europe, Kenya's intelligence service alerted over 1,000 citizens to potential recruitment by Russia for combat in Ukraine. Meanwhile, Boris Johnson called for European allies to send noncombat troops to Ukraine, while Slovakia and Hungary threatened to cut electricity to Ukraine over oil supply issues linked to a drone strike. In the Pacific, the U.S. military conducted a strike against drug traffickers in the eastern Pacific, and the British submarine HMS Anson arrived in Australia for joint exercises under AUKUS. In the South China Sea, maritime cooperation among three partner nations demonstrated regional partnership amid ongoing tensions. In the Middle East, U.S. military buildup near Iran coincided with Israeli airstrikes in Lebanon, resulting in multiple casualties. Additionally, Hezbollah's potential shift in command to Iranian Revolutionary Guards raised concerns, as regional tensions surged following controversial remarks by the U.S. Ambassador to Israel. In Africa, a gunman attack in Nigeria killed at least 50, and South Africa received a credit upgrade, boosting investor confidence amid regional instability.

  • SOUTHCOM: No significant updates found.

  • EUCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Kenya's intelligence service warned over 1,000 citizens may have been recruited under false pretenses to fight for Russia in Ukraine. Former British Prime Minister Boris Johnson urged the U.K. and European allies to deploy noncombat troops to Ukraine to show support against Russian aggression. Slovakia and Hungary threatened to cut off electricity to Ukraine due to halted Russian oil flows, which they claimed were caused by a drone strike on oil transit. Russia warned South Korea against supplying Ukraine with arms, stating it could harm bilateral relations. President Vladimir Zelensky reported that both Russia and the U.S. insisted Ukraine withdraw from the Donbass region. Ukrainian forces sustained combat operations, with Kharkiv reporting two fatalities from a Russian attack. Additionally, Ukrainian drones successfully targeted a major missile factory deep within Russia.

  • PACOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments. The United States military conducted a strike on 27 October that killed three individuals involved in drug trafficking on a vessel in the eastern Pacific Ocean. Concurrently, the British nuclear-powered attack submarine, HMS Anson, arrived in Perth, Australia, to participate in training exercises under the AUKUS partnership. In the South China Sea, three partner nations engaged in a multilateral maritime cooperative activity off the west coast of the Philippine island of Luzon, demonstrating naval collaboration amid ongoing tensions in the region. A Philippine senator visited Thitu Island on 27 October, advocating for strengthened defense ties and urging continual resistance against China’s maritime claims. Additionally, negotiations for a South China Sea code of conduct were scheduled to resume in Singapore in late February, reflecting ongoing diplomatic efforts to manage regional disputes.

  • CENTCOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated significant military and geopolitical developments in the Middle East. President Donald Trump considered options regarding Iran amid a U.S. military buildup, which placed substantial firepower in proximity to the country. On 29 February, Israeli airstrikes in eastern Lebanon resulted in the deaths of at least ten individuals, including eight members of Hezbollah, prompting warnings from the group about potential retaliation. Concurrently, U.S. Ambassador to Israel Mike Huckabee faced backlash from Arab and Muslim nations after claiming Israel had a biblical right to much of the Middle East, a statement that intensified regional tensions. Reports also emerged that Hezbollah may no longer be directed by Lebanese leadership but by Iranian Revolutionary Guards. In addition to these events, former British Prime Minister Boris Johnson advocated for the deployment of noncombat troops to Ukraine, reflecting ongoing concerns about European security dynamics in light of the Russian threat.

  • AFRICOM: During the reporting period, open source reporting indicated that Kenya's intelligence service warned of over 1,000 citizens potentially recruited to fight for Russia in Ukraine, often under false pretenses. In South Africa, the nation achieved its first credit upgrade in two decades as S&P Global Ratings elevated its sovereign rating one notch to double B, leading to increased investor confidence. Meanwhile, a gunman attack in northwest Nigeria resulted in at least 50 fatalities, as reported by a local lawmaker. In South Sudan, the UN Emergency Relief Coordinator visited to assess the humanitarian crisis amid ongoing conflicts between government and opposition forces in Jonglei state.
